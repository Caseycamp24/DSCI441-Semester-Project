Abstract: This proposal presents a data-driven framework to improve scoring efficiency and overall performance in the National Basketball Association (NBA) 
by identifying key factors that influence shot-making success. The analysis focuses on shot distance, court location, and game timing, using logistic regression 
to model the probability of a made shot and bootstrapping techniques to quantify uncertainty and ensure statistical reliability. Shots are categorized into 
close range, mid-range, and three-point zones to evaluate efficiency trade-offs and determine optimal shot selection strategies. The study also examines temporal patterns to 
identify key moments for offensive emphasis or player rest, as well as spatial trends to assess variations in shot effectiveness across players and areas of the court. 
These insights are used to develop baseline game strategies that can adapt to opponents while reinforcing team strengths. Ultimately, the goal is to support better decision-making, 
optimize player performance, and increase win probability through analytically driven basketball strategy. 

Methodology: The methodology combines logistic regression and Bayesian modeling to estimate shot make probability as a function of distance and other key features. 
Bootstrapping is used to repeatedly resample the data and generate confidence intervals, ensuring the stability and reliability of model predictions. 
In addition, a Bayesian approach provides probabilistic predictions and uncertainty quantification through posterior distributions, 
allowing for more informed decision-making in shot selection and game strategy.
